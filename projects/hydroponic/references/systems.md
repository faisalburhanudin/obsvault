# Hydroponic Growing Systems Reference

Source: *DIY Hydroponic Gardens* by Tyler Baras

## Quick Selection Guide

| Your Priority                             | Recommended Systems                                              |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Easiest / Lowest Maintenance              | Bottle Hydroponics, Floating Rafts                               |
| No Electricity Needed                     | Bottle Hydroponics, Floating Rafts, Wicking Bed                  |
| Most Versatile (Any Crop)                 | Flood and Drain                                                  |
| Small Space / Indoor                      | Bottle Hydroponics, Floating Rafts, Media Beds, Vertical Gardens |
| Large Flowering Crops (Tomatoes, Peppers) | Top Drip, Flood and Drain                                        |
| Fastest Growth / High Yields              | Aeroponics                                                       |
| Rooftop / Weight Concerns                 | NFT                                                              |
| **Aquaponics Compatible**                 | **Media Beds (#1), NFT, Floating Rafts, DWC**                    |

## System Details

### 1. Bottle Hydroponics (Kratky Method)
**Page: 42**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Electricity** | Not required |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Small |
| **Growing Media** | Stone wool |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens and herbs (lettuce, basil) |

**Pros:**
- Easiest hydroponic system
- No pumps, no electricity
- Low cost & low maintenance
- Looks great

**Cons:**
- Small scale only
- Not suitable for large flowering crops like tomatoes

---

### 2. Floating Rafts (Deep Water Culture)
**Page: 50**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Electricity** | Optional (aeration optional) |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Small to large |
| **Growing Media** | Stone wool seedling cubes |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens and herbs |
| **Aquaponics** | ✅ Works with biofilter |

**Pros:**
- Very low maintenance
- Can go from transplant to harvest without adding water
- Buoyant platform keeps roots in contact with water as level drops

**Cons:**
- Best for shorter crops with lower oxygen requirements
- Not ideal for large flowering crops without aeration
- For aquaponics: requires separate biofilter to handle fish waste conversion

---

### 3. Wicking Bed
**Page: 60**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Electricity** | Not required |
| **Location** | Outdoors or greenhouse (can be modified for indoors) |
| **Size** | Small to large |
| **Growing Media** | Expanded clay pellets and coco coir chips |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens, herbs, strawberries, short flowering crops |

**Pros:**
- No electricity needed
- Uses capillary action (natural wicking)
- Very versatile, works with various substrates

**Cons:**
- Better for outdoor/greenhouse use

---

### 4. Nutrient Film Technique (NFT)
**Page: 69**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Electricity** | Required |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Medium to large |
| **Growing Media** | Stone wool |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens, herbs, strawberries |
| **Aquaponics** | ✅ Works with solids filtration |

**Pros:**
- Very water-efficient
- Lightweight (great for rooftops)
- Can grow many plants on small reservoir
- Highly customizable designs

**Cons:**
- Requires electricity
- Sensitive to flow rate, channel slope, and channel shape
- Not ideal for beginners
- For aquaponics: requires solids filtration to prevent clogging

### 6. Media Beds
**Page: 92**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Beginner-Intermediate |
| **Electricity** | Required |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Small to large |
| **Growing Media** | Expanded clay pellets |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens, herbs, strawberries, short crops |
| **Aquaponics** | ✅ **Best choice for aquaponics** |

**Pros:**
- Easy to grow wide range of crops
- **Great for aquaponics** - provides lots of surface area for beneficial bacteria that convert fish waste to plant nutrients
- The substrate acts as both physical and biological filter
- Fun, customizable design

**Cons:**
- Limited substrate options
- Difficult to clean
- Root debris accumulates over time

### 7. Flood and Drain (Ebb and Flow)
**Page: 99**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Beginner-Intermediate |
| **Electricity** | Required |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Small to large |
| **Growing Media** | Perlite, expanded clay pellets, stone wool, or coco coir |
| **Best Crops** | **Any crop** (most versatile system) |

**Pros:**
- Most versatile - can grow anything from microgreens to large tomatoes
- Highly customizable
- Works in any location
- Can be modified with different pot sizes, irrigation frequency, flood height

**Cons:**
- Requires electricity (pump and timer)
- Outdoor systems can have issues with rain diluting nutrient solution

---

### 8. Aeroponics
**Page: 106**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Difficulty** | Advanced |
| **Electricity** | Required |
| **Location** | Indoors, outdoors, or greenhouse |
| **Size** | Small to large |
| **Growing Media** | Perlite or clay pellets (or none - roots suspended in air) |
| **Best Crops** | Leafy greens, herbs, strawberries, clones/cuttings |

**Types:**
- **High Pressure**: Uses misters to create fine mist (popular for rooting cuttings/clones)
- **Low Pressure**: Uses perforated disks or splashes (fewer moving parts, less clogging)

**Pros:**
- Potential for very fast growth
- Huge yields
- Uses very little water

**Cons:**
- High-pressure systems prone to clogging
- Top-heavy plants need additional support (no substrate to grip)
- More maintenance than other systems

## Aquaponics Compatibility

Aquaponics combines hydroponics with aquaculture (fish farming). The fish waste is broken down by beneficial bacteria into plant-available nutrients.

### Best Systems for Aquaponics

#### 1. 🥇 Media Beds (Top Choice)
**Page: 93-94**

Media beds are **the most popular system for aquaponics** because:
- The bed acts as both a **physical and biological filter** for fish waste
- Coarse substrate provides plenty of surface area for **beneficial bacteria** that convert fish waste into plant nutrients
- Catches **solid fish waste**, helping keep the fish tank clean
- Works like a living filter - plants clean the water for the fish

**Best Substrates for Aquaponic Media Beds:**
- **Expanded clay pellets** (most popular, also called Hydroton/LECA)
- Expanded shale
- River stone
- Lava rock
- Aquarium gravel
- Drainage gravel

⚠️ **Important:** Always use pH neutral substrate (avoid limestone) and prewash before use.

**Maintenance Note:** Media beds function well for the first few crops but eventually need cleaning. Root systems from harvested crops break down and organic matter accumulates. Substrates can be sterilized, rinsed, and reused.

---

#### 2. NFT (Nutrient Film Technique)
- Can work for aquaponics but requires **solids filtration** before water enters channels
- Fish waste solids can clog NFT channels
- Best for established aquaponic systems with good filtration

---

#### 3. Floating Rafts / Deep Water Culture (DWC)
- Works well for aquaponics
- Requires **separate biofilter** to handle fish waste conversion
- Good for leafy greens and herbs

---

### Why Other Systems Are Less Ideal

| System | Aquaponics Compatibility | Reason |
|--------|-------------------------|--------|
| **Bottle Hydroponics** | ❌ Poor | Static water, no filtration |
| **Wicking Bed** | ⚠️ Limited | Designed for soil-like substrates |
| **Top Drip** | ⚠️ Moderate | Can work but needs filtration |
| **Flood and Drain** | ✅ Good | Similar to media beds with pots |
| **Aeroponics** | ❌ Poor | Misters clog easily with fish waste |
| **Vertical Gardens** | ⚠️ Limited | Complex water distribution |

---

### Key Considerations for Aquaponics

1. **Biological Filtration is Critical**
   - Beneficial bacteria convert ammonia (toxic to fish) → nitrites → nitrates (plant food)
   - This takes 4-6 weeks to establish in a new system (called "cycling")

2. **Nitrogen Source**
   - Fish waste provides nitrogen
   - New aquaponic systems sometimes have nitrogen deficiencies until bacteria colonies establish

3. **Solid Waste Management**
   - Fish produce solid waste that can clog systems
   - Media beds handle solids well (they trap and break down)
   - Other systems need separate solids filters

4. **pH Balance**
   - Fish prefer pH 7.0-8.0
   - Plants prefer pH 5.5-6.5
   - Aquaponic systems typically run at pH 6.8-7.0 as a compromise

5. **Beginner-Friendly**
   - Aquaponics is considered one of the **most beginner-friendly organic hydroponic options**
   - However, you'll be managing both plants AND fish (more complex than pure hydroponics)

## Decision Tree

```
What do you want to grow?
│
├── Leafy greens & herbs only
│   ├── No electricity? → Bottle Hydroponics or Floating Rafts
│   ├── Want simplest? → Floating Rafts
│   └── Want to scale up? → NFT or Media Beds
│
├── Large crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers)
│   ├── Indoor/Small space → Top Drip (Dutch Buckets)
│   └── Maximum versatility → Flood and Drain
│
├── Mix of crops / Unknown
│   └── Best choice → Flood and Drain
│
└── Cuttings/Cloning
    └── Best choice → Aeroponics (high pressure)
```
